Academics

I began my undergraduate career very certain that I would be majoring in biology. In order to get out of a math class, I enrolled in a computer science course during second semester of my freshman year. This "practical" skill was something I soon realized I liked and was actually good at. I could not say the same for biology, as I struggled to memorize every bit of information for the exam. So I am now pursuing a double major in computer science and biology, focusing on the intersection of the two fields, and in particular researching the systems modeling of developmental and regenerative biology. This allows me to continue my passion for biology using the tools and methodologies of computer science that I like the best.
